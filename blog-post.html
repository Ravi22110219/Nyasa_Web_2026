My 4-Year Journey with Nyasa – From Volunteer to Communication Coordinator

Er Ravi Kumawat

Software Development Engineer In AIResQ

BTech From IIT Gandhinagar

Nyasa Communication Team Coordinator (2025-26)

Teaching and learning go hand-in-hand in Nyasa's educational initiatives

When I joined IIT Gandhinagar in 2022 as a first-year Civil Engineering student, I was excited about academics, campus life, and new opportunities. Like many freshers, I was exploring where I belonged outside classrooms — something that would give meaning to my college journey beyond grades and assignments. That search led me to Nyasa, and unknowingly, it became one of the most defining parts of my IIT life.

Looking back today, from my first step as a volunteer to becoming the Communication Team Coordinator in my fourth year, Nyasa has shaped not just my skills but also my perspective towards society, responsibility, leadership, and empathy.

This is not just a story of roles changing every year — it is a story of growth, learning, and finding purpose.

Journey Timeline

First Year Second Year Third Year Fourth Year Conclusion
2022-23

First Year: Beginning as a Volunteer – Discovering Ground Reality

In my very first year at IIT Gandhinagar, I joined the Nyasa Chetana Program as a volunteer. At that time, I did not know how deeply this decision would impact me. Chetana focuses on providing education to construction workers' children and children from nearby villages such as Palaj and Basan.

Key Insight

Teaching isn't just about transferring knowledge—it's about connecting with each student's unique learning journey.

I started teaching Hindi and Music. Initially, I thought teaching would simply mean going there, completing lessons, and returning. But very quickly, I realized that it was much more than that.

"Their excitement to learn even basic concepts made me appreciate the privilege of education I had always taken for granted."

50+ Children Taught
2 Subjects
1 Life-Changing Experience

Music classes were especially special. It was not just about songs — it became a space where children expressed themselves, relaxed, and connected. Over time, I started understanding their backgrounds, struggles, and dreams.

Takeaway: That first year gave me something priceless — ground-level understanding of social outreach. It changed how I saw education, opportunity, and responsibility.

2023-24

Second Year: Deeper Involvement – Expanding My Contribution

In my second year, my connection with Nyasa became stronger. I continued volunteering in Chetana, but this time with more confidence and responsibility. I started understanding how programs are planned, how logistics are managed, and how consistent engagement is maintained.

Along with Chetana, I also volunteered in the Sanjeevani initiative. Through this, I got exposure to another dimension of social outreach — health awareness and support activities.

What I Learned

  • Program planning and logistics management
  • Importance of consistent community engagement
  • Healthcare outreach basics
  • Team coordination essentials

This phase was important because I moved from being someone who just participated to someone who started thinking about impact, sustainability, and continuity of initiatives.

Key Learning: Social work is not just about good intentions. It requires planning, consistency, documentation, and teamwork.

2024-25

Third Year: Transition to Leadership – Joining the Organising Team

In my third year, I joined the Nyasa Organising Team. This was a major transition. Instead of only working on field-level activities, I moved into backend operations — specifically Social Media and Website Team.

Social Media Management

Representing real stories responsibly, showing impact without sensationalizing struggles

Website Development

Combining technical skills with social impact work, helping scale reach and credibility

This role opened a completely new dimension for me. I learned how communication shapes outreach, how digital presence increases impact, how storytelling helps social initiatives reach more people, and how documentation preserves the work done by volunteers.

"Third year was when I started thinking like a team member who contributes to strategy and visibility, not just execution."

2025-26

Fourth Year: Communication Team Coordinator – Leading People and Vision

In my fourth year, I took on the role of Communication Team Coordinator. This was both exciting and challenging. For the first time, I was responsible not just for tasks, but for people, coordination, and long-term communication strategy.

Teams I Managed

Social Media Team

Content creation and engagement

Website Team

Development and maintenance

Editorial Team

Content writing and editing

Design Team

Visual content creation

Total: 16+ team members

Leadership Learnings

  • Managing diverse personalities with different creative approaches
  • Aligning multiple teams toward one communication vision
  • Delegation based on strengths rather than convenience
  • Mentoring juniors and helping them grow
  • Maintaining accountability while encouraging creativity

Biggest Learning: Leadership is not about control — it is about enabling others to perform at their best.

Beyond Roles – Personal Transformation

Empathy

Working with children from different socio-economic backgrounds changed my worldview. It made me more grounded and aware of realities outside campus.

Communication

From teaching children to managing teams — communication became one of my strongest skills.

Leadership

I learned leadership is about listening, guiding, and supporting rather than commanding.

Responsibility

Nyasa taught me that social work is not charity — it is responsibility.

Most Memorable Moments

01

Seeing children improve in confidence over months

Watching shy students become confident speakers was incredibly rewarding.

02

When juniors I mentored took initiative on their own

The pride of seeing team members grow and lead was unmatched.

03

When our communication efforts helped increase participation

Seeing tangible results from our strategies was validating.

04

When team members told me they enjoyed working under my coordination

These moments made every effort worth it.

Message to Juniors and Future Volunteers

If you are thinking about joining social initiatives, I would say — do it.

Not for certificates. Not for resume points. But for perspective. For growth. For understanding the world better.

You will gain skills you never expected:

People Management Communication Emotional Intelligence Real-world Problem Solving Project Planning Team Coordination

And most importantly — you will gain purpose.

What Nyasa Means to Me Today

Today, Nyasa is not just an organization for me. It is a part of my identity.

Social Responsibility

Collective Effort

Grassroots Impact

Youth-driven Change

It showed me that even small consistent efforts can create meaningful change in society. Even after graduation, I hope to stay connected to social impact work in some form. Nyasa has planted that seed permanently in me.

Because once you experience real impact — you cannot ignore it.

Gratitude

I am grateful to the children who trusted us as teachers, the teammates who worked alongside me, the seniors who guided me, the juniors who carried forward the work, and Nyasa, for shaping my IIT journey in the most meaningful way.

Nyasa did not just give me roles.

It gave me direction. It gave me responsibility. It gave me purpose.

And for that, I will always be thankful.
